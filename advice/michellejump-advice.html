Hi my name is Michelle and I have some advice for you!

Experience is key, not a degree!

I've been working for a long time, and I've been looking at how to transition into tech - particularly back-end development.

I thought the best way to get credibility was the long and hard way - doing a degree, part-time, while working full-time and being a caregiver. This would take around seven years, but I got started and optimistically thought that doing my programming papers would lead to a junior developer. It didn't.

Two years, thousands of dollars and hundreds of study hours later, and aggravating employers with the need to take time off around assignment time, I still hadn't got a role in tech.

So I decided to revisit my plans. How to actually get a role in tech and development?

The answer is - practice. Practice, experience, patience and tenacity, over a period of time.

A degree is a wonderful thing, and I will go back to mine, but it may not necessarily be the door to open a new career up.

That's why, after a two year absence, I've come back to Skillcrush, and put my degree on hold for a while.

In two months of a Blueprint, I've learned so, so much more - in much less study time! - than I did spending 20 hours a week studying and burning myself out. :)

I can have a life, have fun learning to code, spend a fraction of the money investing in my education (and have lifetime access to the course content instead of just a year), not burn myself out, and gain valuable experience - all while being flexible and not having to worry about assignment extension requests and taking time off work to study.

So my advice would be - if you want to be a developer, get the practical skills first - degree second! :) Build your portfolio site and Github account, and just keep going!

I'm not going to spend six or seven exhausting years to not get the job I want - I'm going to study with Skillcrush (and others), get career coaching, and get the career I'd love to have. :)

Then, when I'm ready, and have flexibility with my work, I'll go back and finish my degree - because I want to, not because I have to.

Keep going!!! Skillcrush is the perfect starting point to get you firmly on the right path with your new career. It takes time, but you WILL get there! :) And so will I.
